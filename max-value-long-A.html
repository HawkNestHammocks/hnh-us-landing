Chapter One

The problem nobody admits

Or: why every camper secretly resents their tent.

You know the feeling. You've hiked all day, your legs are done, and you reach camp only to pitch a tent on the flattest patch of ground you can find — which isn't flat at all. A rock finds your hip. The cold seeps up through the floor. By 2 AM you're shifting for the hundredth time, wondering why you ever thought this was relaxing.

Here's the dirty secret of camping: sleeping on the ground is objectively miserable. We just accept it because we've never known better.

And ordinary hammocks aren't the answer either. You're bent like a banana, your back aches, and the moment the temperature drops the cold air beneath turns it into a wind tunnel. Add bugs, rain, and zero privacy, and it's a glorified nap — not a shelter.

That exact frustration is where everything started.
