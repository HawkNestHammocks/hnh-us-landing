Chapter 2

When the Mountains Test Your GearRain. Wind. Cold. Repeat.

The first three days were kind. Clear skies, moderate temperatures, well-spaced trees at every campsite. The Hawk Nest went up in four minutes flat. Steve slept like he was in a hotel.

Then day four happened.

A front moved in and stayed. Rain — not drizzle, real rain — hammered the mountains for 36 hours straight. Wind gusts shook the trees. Temperature dropped to near freezing at night.

This is where cheap gear fails and real gear proves itself.

Steve crawled into his Hawk Nest while his hiking partner struggled with a flooded tent floor. The rain fly shed water perfectly. The sleeping pad pocket kept insulation underneath him. Suspended between two spruces, he was above the rivulets of water streaming across the forest floor.

He slept through it. His partner did not.
