Chapter 1

The Problem Nobody Talks AboutOr: why every camper secretly hates their tent

You know the feeling. You've hiked all day. Your legs are done. You reach camp, set up your tent on the flattest patch of ground you can find — which isn't flat at all — and crawl inside.

A rock digs into your hip. The ground is cold. It rained yesterday, so there's moisture seeping through the floor. By 2 AM you're shifting positions for the hundredth time, staring at the ceiling, wondering why you thought this was fun.

Here's the dirty secret of camping: ground sleeping is objectively terrible. We just accept it because we don't know any better.

Traditional hammocks aren't much better. You're curved like a banana, your back is screaming, and if the temperature drops below 50°F, the cold air beneath you turns the hammock into a wind tunnel. Plus — bugs, rain, no privacy. It's a glorified nap, not a shelter.

This was the exact frustration that started everything.
